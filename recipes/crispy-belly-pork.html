Crispy Belly Pork

Description

I LOVE me some crispy pork belly, I mean, who doesn’t? But if you’ve ever tried to make the Chinese style crispy pork belly with the bubbly skin, you know it’s not a quick and easy thing. Even with my recipe, which is one of the simplest out there, it's a 2-day process.

So THIS recipe, is much simpler. It's a Thai style fried pork belly that is unbelievably good, and it's only going to take you 30 minutes. I made this for a Thai friend, and after one bite and he said, "You need to sell this!" It was THAT good!

Ingredients

  • Pork Belly
  • Fish Sauce
  • Sugar
  • Ground white pepper
  • Cornstarch
  • All-purpose flour

Steps

  1. Pat the pork belly dry as much as possible with a paper towel.
  2. Cut the pork into 1-inch wide strips, and then cut each strip into pieces about ¾-inch thick.
  3. Place the pork into a large mixing bowl and add the fish sauce, sugar, and white pepper. Use your hand to massage the pork and mix all the seasonings.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and the cornstarch and mix well. Sprinkle the flour mixture over the pork and mix well, making sure none of the pieces are sticking together.
  5. Add at least 1 inch of frying oil to a wok or a medium pot and heat it to 350°F (175°C) for the first fry.
  6. First Fry: Add half of the pork, placing it in the hot oil one piece at a time to ensure they are not sticking together. Fry for 2 minutes (time this).
  7. Remove the pork promptly and place on a plate to cool until it’s no more than lukewarm before frying them a second time.
  8. Second Fry: Once the pork is cool, bring the oil temp up to 385°F(196°C) over high heat. Add the pork all at once and fry for 70-90 seconds, or until the exterior is a deep golden brown.
  9. Allow the pork to cool for a few minutes before digging in as the fatty part of the pork is VERY hot out of the fryer!!
